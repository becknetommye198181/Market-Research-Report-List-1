<p><p>The rheology modifiers market is primarily divided into organic and inorganic types. Organic rheology modifiers are typically derived from natural or synthetic polymers, such as cellulose and acrylates, and are used to enhance viscosity, stability, and flow properties in various applications, including paints, coatings, and cosmetics. In contrast, inorganic rheology modifiers, like clays and silicates, are mineral-based and primarily serve to thicken and stabilize formulations, offering improved performance in industrial sectors such as oil and gas, construction, and personal care.</p></p>
